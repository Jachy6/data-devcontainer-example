Data Description

The Wine Quality dataset is available on Kaggle and can be found at the following URL:

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rajyellow46/wine-quality

This dataset contains information about different wines, including their physical and chemical properties, as well as their quality rating. The dataset is divided into two categories: red wine and white wine. The data was originally sourced from the UCI Machine Learning Repository.

The variables in the dataset are:

  1. type: The type of wine (red or white).
  2. fixed acidity: The amount of non-volatile acids present in the wine (measured in g/dm³).
  3. volatile acidity: The amount of acetic acid in the wine, which can give an undesirable vinegar taste if present in high concentrations (measured in g/dm³).
  4. citric acid: A weak organic acid found in citrus fruits, which can add freshness and flavor to the wine (measured in g/dm³).
  5. residual sugar: The amount of sugar remaining in the wine after fermentation (measured in g/dm³).
  6. chlorides: The amount of salt present in the wine (measured in g/dm³).
  7. free sulfur dioxide: The amount of free sulfur dioxide (SO2) in the wine, which can act as an antioxidant and antimicrobial agent (measured in mg/dm³).
  8. total sulfur dioxide: The total amount of sulfur dioxide (SO2) in the wine, including both free and bound forms (measured in mg/dm³).
  9. density: The density of the wine, which is affected by the concentration of alcohol and sugar (measured in g/cm³).
  10. pH: A measure of the acidity or basicity of the wine, where lower values indicate higher acidity and higher values indicate more basic wines. The scale ranges from 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very basic), with 7 being neutral.
  11. sulphates: The amount of potassium sulphate (K2SO4) in the wine, which can contribute to the wine’s overall taste and aroma (measured in g/dm³).
  12. alcohol: The percentage of alcohol by volume in the wine.
  13. quality: The quality rating of the wine, assigned by wine experts on a scale of 0 (low quality) to 10 (high quality).
